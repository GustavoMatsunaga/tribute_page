Japanese Immigration to Brazil

Even a journey with a thousand mile starts with a small step

Here's a time line of the Japanese Immigration to Brazil:

  • 1894 - Tadashi Nemoto, deputy from Japan, visits Brazil.
  • 1907 - Brazil publishes the Immigration and Colonization laws, allowing each state to define its own way to receive and install the immigrants.
  • 1908 - Kasato Maru, the first ship, arrives in Brazil.
  • 1910 - Ryojun Maru, the second ship, arrives in Brazil with 906 workers.
  • 1911 - The first 05 families of immigrants to buy their own property.
  • 1914 - São Paulo had more then 10 thousand immigrants with a financial problem, so they decided to stop the immigration process.
  • 1918 - Brazil build in Rio de Janeiro the first two schools for the japanese immigrants.
  • 1938 - The govern decided to restrain the immigrants activities and close all the schools for immigrants.
  • 1948 - Yukishige Tamura is elected the first japanese councilor in São Paulo, becoming the first Nikkey to hold and elective office in a capital.
  • 1949 - Trades between Brazil and Japan are restored through a bilateral agreement.
  • 1953 - Brazil approves a project to introduce 5,000 immigrant families into the country. Japanese companies are encouraged to start planning investments in Brazil. The first of them arrived in 1953.
  • 1967 - Crown Prince Akihito and Princess Michiko visit Brazil for the first time.
  • 1973 - Arrive in Santos the last ship, Nippon Maru.
